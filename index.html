Most Interesting Serial Killers

  • Giulia Tofana

  • H.H. Holmes

    • Henry Howard Holmes, also known as H.H. Holmes, was a British serial killer
    and is known as one of the "first" serial killers.
    Holmes was active from 1891 to 1894.
    He operated a hotel that he later turned into an elaborate torture maze.

  • Zodiac Killer

    • The Zodiac Killer was an American serial killer who the police were unable to identify.
    He was located in Northern California and was active through 1968 and 1969.
    His claim to fame were his encrypted letters sent directly to the police to taunt them.

  • Ed Gein

    • Ed Gein, also known as the Butcher of Plainfield, was another American serial killer who was based in Wisconsin.
    He was active for a decade through the years 1947 to 1957.
    Ed Gein is most known for skinning human corpses and creating furniture and other creations from them.

  • Elizabeth Bathory

    • Elizabeth Bathory, also known as the "Blood Countess", was a Hungarian noblewoman and an alleged serial killer.
    Bathory was active throughout the years of 1590 to 1610.
    Elizabeth Bathory is most known for torturing and killing hundred of young girls and women and bathing in their blood.
    She believed bathing in her victim's blood would keep her youthful.

  • John Wayne Gacy

    • John Wayne Gacy, also know as Pogo the Clown, was an American serial killer based in Illinois.
    He was active throughout the years 1972 to 1978.
    Gacy's claim to fame was the fact that he was a clown for children's birthday parties.
    His victims consisted of young boys, most of which he tortured.
